What impact do CO2 and VOCs have on my home air quality?
CO2 (carbon dioxide) is an odorless gas which occurs naturally as a waste product of respiration as well as from the decomposition of organic materials. There is no lower limit for CO2, but high levels (above 1000ppm) can cause headaches, fatigue, and difficulty in beathing.
VOCs (volatile organic compounds) are gases which are released from a variety of sources such as paint thinners, aerosol sprays, dry cleaned clothing, pesiticides, automotive equipment and more. There is no lower threshold for VOCs indoors, but The US Green Building Council advises that it should be kept below 500ng/L as above that there is great risk for skin and lung conditions.
